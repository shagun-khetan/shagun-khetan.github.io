excerpt: 'We examine the effect of affordable high-speed mobile internet on children’s educational outcomes using the nationwide rollout of Reliance Jio’s 4G network in India in 2016 as a natural experiment. Jio’s entry led to an abrupt and unanticipated decline in mobile data prices and a rapid expansion of 4G coverage. Exploiting cross-district variation in early exposure to Jio’s network, we implement a difference-in-differences design using district-level test score data from the Annual Status of Education Report (ASER). We find that districts with greater initial access to low-cost 4G internet experienced statistically significant improvements of approximately 0.18 standard deviations in reading scores and 0.15 standard deviations in mathematics scores between 2016 and 2018. These effects are robust to alternative treatment definitions, a continuous treatment-intensity specification, and controls for local economic activity and demographic composition. Our results suggest that widespread access to affordable, mobile-first internet can complement traditional education inputs and improve learning outcomes in developing-country contexts.'
